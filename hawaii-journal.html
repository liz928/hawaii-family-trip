Family Travel Journal

Hawaiʻi
2026

The Zhou Family  ·  July 6–21, 2026  ·  Kauaʻi → the Big Island
01Monday, July 6

Arrival on Kauaʻi

The getting-here day. A rough start — a four-hour delay in Denver stretched an already-long journey, and this came just after all three children had recovered from COVID the week before. Everyone rallied only just in time.

We landed at Lihuʻe around 6:45 in the evening, collected the car, and settled into our three-bedroom residence at Timbers Kauaʻi.

For dinner we kept things simple and ordered takeout from Hualani’s, the oceanfront, farm-to-table restaurant at the resort — a calm close to a very long day of travel.

02Tuesday, July 7

Chocolate, snow cones, and the pool

A well-earned slow day. The air tour was postponed to Thursday for aircraft maintenance, which turned today into a proper settle-in and recovery day. We woke to rain, but it cleared quickly before we left the house, giving way to a sunny afternoon in the upper eighties.

BreakfastA generous start at Tip Top Café in Lihuʻe. Annabelle had the oxtail soup and rated it a perfect ten — highly recommended.

ChocolateWe visited the Lydgate Chocolate Tasting Room in downtown Kapaʻa. The tasting was lovely; our only wish was that we could have done the full farm tour, so the children might better understand how chocolate is actually made. For today, this was just right — perhaps we’ll have that fuller experience when we travel to Costa Rica later. We brought home some chocolate as a souvenir.

Snow conesA few doors down we found Honu Shave Ice — everyone’s favorite stop of the day. Annabelle chose the root beer float, which became the whole family’s favorite; the piña colada was a touch too sweet. Andrew and Adalyn each picked their own flavors.

ProvisionsWe made a run to Costco in Lihuʻe to stock the residence for the week — breakfast items, fruit, and snacks for the children and the pool days ahead.

The poolWe explored both pools — the one just outside our room and the infinity pool by the lobby — and the children preferred the pool in front of our room. It was heated and felt wonderful. The keiki jumping rock was the highlight: roughly an eight-foot jump into five feet of water. Annabelle stayed close to help Andrew reach the side at first, but by the end he was jumping and swimming to the stairs entirely on his own. Poolside, piña coladas and truffle fries.

DinnerWe tried Hawaii Sushi Station, a food truck. The fish was fresh, though the portions were not especially generous; the flavor was good and the salad dressing excellent, if a little spicy for the amount served. Having had a large breakfast, a few snacks, and poolside fries, we skipped lunch altogether and made this our dinner.

Adalyn and Andrew built Andrew a third bed on the floor between their two beds — a pile of pillows under a blanket — and that is where he insisted on sleeping tonight.
