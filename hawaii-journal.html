A Family Travel Journal

Hawaii 2026

The Zhou Family · July 6–21, 2026
Kauaʻi to the Big Island
Day One · Monday, July 6

Arrival on Kauaʻi

The long way to paradise

The getting-here day. A rough start — a four-hour delay in Denver stretched an already-long journey, and this came just after all three children had recovered from COVID the week before. Everyone rallied only just in time.

We finally landed at Lihuʻe around 6:45 in the evening, collected the car, and settled into our three-bedroom residence at Timbers Kauaʻi.

For dinner we kept things simple and ordered takeout from Hualani’s Restaurant, the oceanfront, farm-to-table restaurant at the resort — a calm close to a very long travel day.

Day Two · Tuesday, July 7

Chocolate, Snow Cones & the Pool

A quiet, well-earned day

A well-earned slow day. The Air Ventures air tour was postponed to Thursday for aircraft maintenance, which turned today into a proper settle-in and recovery day.

We woke to rain, but it cleared quickly before we left the house, giving way to a sunny afternoon in the upper eighties.

Breakfast was at Tip Top Café in Lihuʻe — a wonderful spot, and a generous start to the day. Annabelle had the oxtail soup and rated it a perfect ten. Highly recommended.

From there we visited the Lydgate Chocolate Tasting Room in downtown Kapaʻa. The tasting was lovely; our only wish was that we could have done the full farm tour so the children might better understand how chocolate is actually made. For today, though, this was just right — perhaps we’ll have that fuller experience when we travel to Costa Rica later. We brought home some chocolate as a souvenir.

A few doors down we found a snow cone shop — everyone’s favorite stop of the day. Annabelle chose the root beer float, which became the whole family’s favorite; the piña colada was a touch too sweet. Andrew and Adalyn each picked their own flavors. (Shop name to be confirmed for a map link.)

We also made a run to Costco in Lihuʻe to stock the residence for the week — breakfast items, fruit, and snacks for the children and the pool days ahead.

In the afternoon we explored both pools — the one just outside our room and the infinity pool by the lobby and restaurant — and the children preferred the pool in front of our room. It was heated and felt wonderful. The keiki jumping rock was the highlight of the day: roughly an eight-foot jump into five feet of water. Annabelle stayed close to help Andrew reach the side at first, but by the end he was jumping and swimming to the stairs entirely on his own. Poolside, we enjoyed piña coladas and truffle fries.

For dinner we tried Hawaii Sushi Station, a food truck. The fish was fresh, though the portions were not especially generous; the flavor was good and the salad dressing excellent, if a little spicy for the amount served. Having had a large breakfast, a few snacks, and poolside fries, we had skipped lunch altogether and made this our dinner.

A closing note for the day: Adalyn and Andrew built Andrew a third bed on the floor between their two beds — a pile of pillows under a blanket — and that is where he insisted on sleeping tonight.
